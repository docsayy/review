Acute tubular necrosis is the most common cause of acute kidney injury.

Acute tubular necrosis is caused by ischemia or toxins.

Ischemic acute tubular necrosis results from hypotension or shock.

Nephrotoxic acute tubular necrosis is caused by drugs or contrast agents.

Damaged tubular cells slough into the lumen and cause obstruction.

Interstitial nephritis is inflammation of the kidney interstitium.

Drug hypersensitivity is a common cause of interstitial nephritis.

Interstitial nephritis presents with fever, rash, and eosinophilia.
