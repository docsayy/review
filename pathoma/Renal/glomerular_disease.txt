Glomerulonephritis is inflammation of the glomeruli.

Nephritic syndrome presents with hematuria, hypertension, and mild proteinuria.

Nephrotic syndrome presents with heavy proteinuria, hypoalbuminemia, and edema.

Poststreptococcal glomerulonephritis follows group A streptococcal infection.

Poststreptococcal glomerulonephritis shows subepithelial immune complex deposits.

IgA nephropathy presents with episodic hematuria after respiratory infection.

Membranous nephropathy is a common cause of nephrotic syndrome in adults.

Minimal change disease is a common cause of nephrotic syndrome in children.

Focal segmental glomerulosclerosis is associated with HIV and heroin use.
