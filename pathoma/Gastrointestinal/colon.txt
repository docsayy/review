The colon absorbs water and electrolytes.

Ulcerative colitis affects the colon and rectum.

Ulcerative colitis causes continuous mucosal inflammation.

Ulcerative colitis produces crypt abscesses.

Ulcerative colitis increases risk of colorectal carcinoma.

Crohn disease can involve the colon.

Crohn disease causes transmural inflammation and fistula formation.

Diverticulosis is herniation of mucosa through muscular wall.

Diverticulitis is inflammation of diverticula.

Diverticulitis may cause perforation and abscess.

Adenomatous polyps are precancerous lesions.

Colorectal carcinoma commonly arises from adenomatous polyps.

Right-sided colon cancer causes occult bleeding and anemia.

Left-sided colon cancer causes obstruction and pencil-thin stools.
