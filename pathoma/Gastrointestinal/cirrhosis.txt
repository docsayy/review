Cirrhosis is irreversible fibrosis of the liver with regenerative nodules.

Cirrhosis disrupts normal liver architecture.

Cirrhosis causes portal hypertension.

Portal hypertension causes ascites.

Portal hypertension causes splenomegaly.

Portal hypertension leads to portosystemic shunts.

Esophageal varices result from portosystemic shunting.

Ruptured esophageal varices cause massive hematemesis.

Cirrhosis causes decreased synthesis of clotting factors.

Cirrhosis causes hypoalbuminemia.

Cirrhosis increases risk of hepatocellular carcinoma.
