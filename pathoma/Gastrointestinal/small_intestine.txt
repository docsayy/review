The small intestine is the primary site of nutrient absorption.

Celiac disease is autoimmune intolerance to gluten.

Gluten exposure in celiac disease causes villous atrophy.

Celiac disease causes malabsorption and diarrhea.

Celiac disease is associated with dermatitis herpetiformis.

Tropical sprue causes villous atrophy and malabsorption.

Lactase deficiency causes osmotic diarrhea after dairy intake.

Whipple disease is caused by Tropheryma whipplei.

Whipple disease causes malabsorption and weight loss.

Crohn disease commonly affects the terminal ileum.

Crohn disease causes transmural inflammation and skip lesions.
