Chronic pancreatitis is progressive fibrosis and loss of pancreatic function.

Alcohol is the most common cause of chronic pancreatitis.

Chronic pancreatitis causes exocrine pancreatic insufficiency.

Exocrine insufficiency causes malabsorption and steatorrhea.

Chronic pancreatitis can cause diabetes mellitus.

Pancreatic calcifications are seen in chronic pancreatitis.
