Hepatitis is inflammation of the liver.

Viral hepatitis causes hepatocyte necrosis and inflammation.

Hepatitis A is transmitted by fecal-oral route.

Hepatitis A does not cause chronic infection.

Hepatitis B is transmitted by blood and body fluids.

Hepatitis B can cause chronic hepatitis.

Hepatitis C is transmitted primarily through blood.

Hepatitis C frequently progresses to chronic hepatitis.

Chronic hepatitis can lead to cirrhosis.

Ground-glass hepatocytes are seen in hepatitis B.
