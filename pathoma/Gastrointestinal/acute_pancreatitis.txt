Acute pancreatitis is inflammation of the pancreas due to premature activation of digestive enzymes.

Gallstones are the most common cause of acute pancreatitis.

Alcohol is a major cause of acute pancreatitis.

Acute pancreatitis presents with severe epigastric pain radiating to the back.

Serum lipase is the most sensitive marker of acute pancreatitis.

Trypsin activation leads to autodigestion of pancreatic tissue.

Fat necrosis occurs in acute pancreatitis.

Hypocalcemia may occur due to fat saponification.

Severe pancreatitis can cause systemic inflammatory response and shock.
