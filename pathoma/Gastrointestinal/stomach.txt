Parietal cells produce hydrochloric acid and intrinsic factor.

Chief cells produce pepsinogen.

Mucous cells produce protective mucus.

Autoimmune gastritis destroys parietal cells.

Autoimmune gastritis causes loss of intrinsic factor and vitamin B12 deficiency.

Helicobacter pylori infection causes chronic gastritis.

H. pylori damages mucosa by urease activity and inflammation.

H. pylori infection increases risk of gastric adenocarcinoma.

H. pylori infection increases risk of MALT lymphoma.

Peptic ulcer disease results from mucosal injury by acid and pepsin.

NSAIDs cause ulcers by inhibiting prostaglandin synthesis.

Stress ulcers can occur in critically ill patients.

Gastric adenocarcinoma may present with weight loss and anemia.

Linitis plastica is diffuse gastric adenocarcinoma causing rigid stomach.

Virchow node is left supraclavicular lymphadenopathy from gastric cancer metastasis.
