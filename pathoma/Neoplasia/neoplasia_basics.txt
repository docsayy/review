Neoplasia is monoclonal, unregulated, irreversible cell growth.

Hyperplasia is polyclonal and regulated cell growth.

Carcinoma in situ is dysplasia involving the full thickness of epithelium.

Desmoplasia is tumor-induced fibrous stroma formation.

Monoclonality can be determined by G6PD isoenzyme analysis.

Monoclonality in lymphomas is determined by light chain restriction.

A Kappa to Lambda ratio different from 3:1 suggests monoclonality.
