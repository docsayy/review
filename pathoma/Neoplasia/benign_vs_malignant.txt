Benign tumors remain localized and do not metastasize.

Malignant tumors invade locally and can metastasize.

Benign tumors grow slowly.

Malignant tumors grow rapidly.

Benign tumors are well circumscribed.

Malignant tumors are infiltrative.

Benign tumors are mobile.

Malignant tumors are fixed to surrounding tissue.

Adenoma is a benign epithelial tumor forming glands.

Adenocarcinoma is a malignant gland-forming tumor.

Papilloma is a benign papillary epithelial tumor.

Papillary carcinoma is a malignant papillary epithelial tumor.

Lipoma is a benign tumor of fat.

Liposarcoma is a malignant tumor of fat.

Chondroma is a benign cartilage tumor.

Chondrosarcoma is a malignant cartilage tumor.

Osteoma is a benign bone tumor.

Osteosarcoma is a malignant bone tumor.

Nevus is a benign melanocyte tumor.

Melanoma is a malignant melanocyte tumor.

Lymphocytes form lymphomas rather than benign tumors.
