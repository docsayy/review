Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of adult death.

Cancer is the second leading cause of adult death.

Accidents are the leading cause of childhood death.

Prostate cancer is the most common cancer in men.

Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women.

Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer death in both sexes.

Colorectal cancer is common in both sexes.

Ovarian and pancreatic cancers present late due to space for silent growth.

Cancer screening aims to detect dysplasia or early carcinoma.

Pap smear screens for cervical dysplasia.

Mammography screens for breast carcinoma.

PSA and DRE screen for prostate carcinoma.

Colonoscopy screens for colorectal carcinoma.
