Loss of nucleus is the morphologic hallmark of cell death.

Pyknosis is nuclear shrinkage.

Karyorrhexis is nuclear fragmentation.

Karyolysis is nuclear dissolution.

Necrosis is cell death from acute injury and always pathologic.

Coagulative necrosis preserves tissue architecture.

Ischemic infarction of solid organs causes coagulative necrosis except in brain.

Liquefactive necrosis is enzymatic digestion of dead cells.

Brain infarction causes liquefactive necrosis.

Dry gangrene is coagulative necrosis of extremities.

Wet gangrene is liquefactive necrosis with superimposed infection.

Caseous necrosis has cheese-like appearance in TB and fungal infections.

Fat necrosis shows chalky white deposits from saponification.

Fibrinoid necrosis is necrosis of blood vessel walls.
