Regeneration replaces damaged tissue with native tissue.

Repair replaces damaged tissue with scar.

Labile tissues continuously regenerate.

Stable tissues regenerate when needed.

Permanent tissues cannot regenerate.

Granulation tissue contains fibroblasts, capillaries, and myofibroblasts.

Type III collagen is deposited in granulation tissue.

Type I collagen replaces type III collagen in scars.

Vitamin C is required for collagen hydroxylation.

Copper is required for collagen cross-linking.

Zinc is required for collagen remodeling.

Primary intention healing has minimal scarring.

Secondary intention healing has larger scars and wound contraction.

Keloids are excessive type III collagen scars.

Hypertrophic scars are excessive type I collagen scars.
