Hyperplasia is an increase in cell number.

Metaplasia is a change in cell type due to reprogramming of stem cells.

Dysplasia is disordered growth of precancerous cells.

Hyperplasia, metaplasia, and dysplasia are reversible processes.

Pathologic hyperplasia can progress to dysplasia and cancer.

Benign prostatic hyperplasia does not increase cancer risk.

Metaplasia usually affects surface epithelium.

Barrett esophagus is metaplasia from squamous to columnar epithelium.

Metaplasia can progress to dysplasia and cancer.

Apocrine metaplasia in the breast does not increase cancer risk.

Vitamin A deficiency can cause metaplasia.

Dysplasia arises from longstanding hyperplasia or metaplasia.

Persistent dysplasia can progress to carcinoma.

Atrophy is a decrease in cell size or number.

Aplasia is failure of cell production during embryogenesis.

Hypoplasia is decreased cell production during embryogenesis.
