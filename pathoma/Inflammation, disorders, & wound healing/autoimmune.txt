Autoimmune disease results from loss of self-tolerance.

Systemic lupus erythematosus involves type II and type III hypersensitivity.

ANA is sensitive for lupus.

Anti-dsDNA is specific for lupus.

Hydralazine, procainamide, and isoniazid can cause drug-induced lupus.

Antihistone antibodies indicate drug-induced lupus.

Antiphospholipid antibodies cause hypercoagulability.

Lupus anticoagulant prolongs PTT but increases thrombosis risk.

Sjogren syndrome causes autoimmune destruction of salivary and lacrimal glands.

Sjogren syndrome presents with dry eyes and dry mouth.

Anti-SSA and anti-SSB antibodies diagnose Sjogren syndrome.

Scleroderma causes fibroblast activation and collagen deposition.

Diffuse scleroderma is diagnosed by anti-SCL-70 antibody.

Limited scleroderma is diagnosed by anti-centromere antibody.

Mixed connective tissue disease is diagnosed by anti-U1 RNP antibodies.
