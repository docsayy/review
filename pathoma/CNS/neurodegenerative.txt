Alzheimer disease is the most common cause of dementia.

Alzheimer disease shows beta-amyloid plaques.

Alzheimer disease shows neurofibrillary tangles of tau protein.

Parkinson disease is caused by loss of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra.

Lewy bodies are characteristic of Parkinson disease.

Huntington disease is caused by CAG trinucleotide repeat expansion.

Huntington disease causes caudate nucleus atrophy.
