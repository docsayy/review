Iron deficiency is the most common cause of microcytic anemia.

Iron deficiency anemia is caused by chronic blood loss.

Iron deficiency anemia shows low ferritin and high total iron binding capacity.

Sideroblastic anemia is caused by defective heme synthesis.

Sideroblastic anemia shows ring sideroblasts in bone marrow.

Lead poisoning can cause sideroblastic anemia.

Thalassemia is caused by decreased globin chain synthesis.

Beta thalassemia major presents in infancy with severe anemia.

Alpha thalassemia severity depends on number of gene deletions.
