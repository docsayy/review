The coagulation cascade forms fibrin clots.

Hemophilia A is caused by factor VIII deficiency.

Hemophilia B is caused by factor IX deficiency.

Hemophilia is X-linked recessive.

Disseminated intravascular coagulation causes widespread clotting and bleeding.

Vitamin K deficiency reduces synthesis of clotting factors II, VII, IX, and X.

Liver disease causes decreased clotting factor production.
