An embolus is a detached intravascular clot or material that travels in blood.

Pulmonary embolism usually arises from deep vein thrombosis.

Pulmonary embolism causes ventilation-perfusion mismatch.

Large pulmonary emboli cause acute right heart strain.

Fat embolism can occur after long bone fractures.

Amniotic fluid embolism occurs during childbirth.

Air embolism can occur from intravenous lines.

Systemic arterial emboli often arise from left heart thrombi.
