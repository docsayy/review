Thrombosis is formation of a blood clot inside a vessel.

Virchow triad describes factors promoting thrombosis.

Virchow triad includes endothelial injury.

Virchow triad includes abnormal blood flow.

Virchow triad includes hypercoagulability.

Atherosclerosis causes endothelial injury and thrombosis.

Turbulent flow promotes thrombosis.

Stasis promotes thrombosis.

Factor V Leiden mutation causes hypercoagulability.

Prothrombin mutation causes hypercoagulability.

Antiphospholipid antibodies cause hypercoagulability.

Cancer increases risk of thrombosis.
