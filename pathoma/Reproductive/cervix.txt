The cervix is lined by squamous epithelium externally and glandular epithelium internally.

The transformation zone is the junction of squamous and glandular epithelium.

HPV infection targets the transformation zone.

HPV 16 and 18 are high-risk types for cervical cancer.

Cervical dysplasia is detected by Pap smear.

Koilocytes are squamous cells with perinuclear clearing caused by HPV.

Cervical carcinoma spreads locally before distant metastasis.
