Rheumatic fever follows group A streptococcal pharyngitis.

Rheumatic fever is caused by molecular mimicry between streptococcal antigens and cardiac tissue.

Aschoff bodies are granulomatous lesions seen in rheumatic fever.

Aschoff bodies contain T cells, plasma cells, and activated macrophages.

Rheumatic heart disease most commonly affects the mitral valve.

Rheumatic mitral stenosis shows commissural fusion and thickened valve leaflets.

Rheumatic fever can also cause aortic valve disease.
