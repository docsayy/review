Infective endocarditis is infection of the endocardial surface of the heart.

Endocarditis most commonly affects cardiac valves.

Vegetations consist of fibrin, platelets, and microorganisms.

Acute endocarditis is most commonly caused by Staphylococcus aureus.

Acute endocarditis can infect normal valves.

Subacute endocarditis is most commonly caused by viridans streptococci.

Subacute endocarditis usually infects previously abnormal valves.

IV drug use predisposes to tricuspid valve endocarditis.

Endocarditis can cause septic emboli.

Endocarditis can cause immune complex glomerulonephritis.
