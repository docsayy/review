Ischemic heart disease results from reduced coronary blood flow.

Stable angina occurs with exertion and resolves with rest.

Unstable angina occurs at rest and indicates plaque rupture.

Myocardial infarction is irreversible myocardial necrosis.

ST elevation MI indicates full-thickness infarction.

Troponin is the most sensitive marker of myocardial injury.

Coagulative necrosis occurs in myocardial infarction.

Macrophages clear necrotic myocardium after infarction.

Granulation tissue forms during infarct healing.

Scar tissue replaces necrotic myocardium.
