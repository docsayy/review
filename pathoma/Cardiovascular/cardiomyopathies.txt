Dilated cardiomyopathy causes dilation of cardiac chambers and systolic dysfunction.

Dilated cardiomyopathy may be caused by alcohol toxicity.

Dilated cardiomyopathy may be caused by viral myocarditis.

Doxorubicin chemotherapy can cause dilated cardiomyopathy.

Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy is caused by autosomal dominant mutations in sarcomere proteins.

Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy causes asymmetric septal hypertrophy.

Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy can cause dynamic outflow obstruction.

Restrictive cardiomyopathy causes impaired ventricular filling with preserved systolic function.

Amyloidosis is a common cause of restrictive cardiomyopathy.
