Aneurysm is localized dilation of a blood vessel due to wall weakness.

Atherosclerosis is the most common cause of abdominal aortic aneurysm.

Abdominal aortic aneurysms are most often infrarenal.

Aortic dissection occurs when blood enters the media through an intimal tear.

Hypertension is the most important risk factor for aortic dissection.

Marfan syndrome predisposes to aortic dissection due to cystic medial degeneration.

Temporal arteritis causes headache and jaw claudication.

Temporal arteritis is associated with polymyalgia rheumatica.

Takayasu arteritis affects the aortic arch and major branches.

Polyarteritis nodosa causes transmural necrotizing vasculitis of medium arteries.

Kawasaki disease can cause coronary artery aneurysms in children.
