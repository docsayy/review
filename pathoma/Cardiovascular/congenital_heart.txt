Ventricular septal defect is the most common congenital heart defect.

Ventricular septal defect initially causes left-to-right shunt.

Large ventricular septal defects may progress to Eisenmenger syndrome.

Eisenmenger syndrome is reversal to right-to-left shunt due to pulmonary hypertension.

Atrial septal defect causes fixed splitting of the second heart sound.

Patent ductus arteriosus causes continuous machinery-like murmur.

Maternal rubella infection is associated with patent ductus arteriosus.

Prostaglandin E maintains ductus arteriosus patency.

NSAIDs close a patent ductus arteriosus.
