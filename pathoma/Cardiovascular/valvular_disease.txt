Aortic stenosis causes left ventricular hypertrophy.

Calcific degeneration is the most common cause of aortic stenosis.

Bicuspid aortic valve predisposes to early aortic stenosis.

Mitral regurgitation causes left atrial dilation.

Mitral valve prolapse is caused by myxomatous degeneration.

Tricuspid regurgitation is commonly due to right ventricular dilation.
