Obstructive lung disease causes increased airway resistance.

Asthma is reversible airway obstruction.

Asthma involves bronchoconstriction and mucus plugging.

Eosinophils play a key role in asthma.

COPD is irreversible airflow limitation.

Chronic bronchitis causes mucus gland enlargement.

Emphysema causes destruction of alveolar walls.

Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency causes panacinar emphysema.

Smoking is the major cause of COPD.
