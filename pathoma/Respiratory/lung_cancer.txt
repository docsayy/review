Lung cancer is strongly associated with smoking.

Squamous cell carcinoma arises from bronchial epithelium.

Squamous cell carcinoma may produce keratin pearls.

Squamous cell carcinoma is associated with hypercalcemia.

Adenocarcinoma is the most common lung cancer in nonsmokers.

Adenocarcinoma often arises in peripheral lung tissue.

Small cell carcinoma is a neuroendocrine tumor.

Small cell carcinoma produces paraneoplastic syndromes.

Large cell carcinoma is an undifferentiated malignant lung tumor.
