Disseminated intravascular coagulation causes consumption of platelets and clotting factors.

Bleeding time, PT, and PTT are all increased in DIC.

D-dimer is increased in DIC.

Fibrinogen is decreased in DIC.

Schistocytes are seen on peripheral smear in DIC.

Hemolytic uremic syndrome presents with thrombocytopenia, hemolytic anemia, and renal failure.

Thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura presents with HUS features plus fever and neurologic symptoms.

TTP is caused by ADAMTS13 deficiency.

Treatment of TTP is plasmapheresis.
