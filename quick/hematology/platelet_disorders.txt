Immune thrombocytopenic purpura is caused by antibodies against platelet glycoprotein IIb/IIIa.

Platelet count is decreased in immune thrombocytopenic purpura.

Bleeding time is increased in immune thrombocytopenic purpura.

PT and PTT are normal in immune thrombocytopenic purpura.

Typical presentation is a child after viral illness with petechiae.

Treatment is steroids, then IVIG, then splenectomy.

Von Willebrand disease is autosomal dominant.

Von Willebrand factor mediates platelet adhesion.

Bleeding time is increased in von Willebrand disease.

Platelet count is normal in von Willebrand disease.

PT is normal in von Willebrand disease.

PTT may be increased in von Willebrand disease.

Desmopressin increases von Willebrand factor levels.
