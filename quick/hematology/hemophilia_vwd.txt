Hemophilia A is factor VIII deficiency.

Hemophilia B is factor IX deficiency.

Both are X-linked recessive.

PTT is prolonged in hemophilia.

PT and bleeding time are normal in hemophilia.

Classic presentation is hemarthrosis in a young boy.

Hemophilia A is treated with factor VIII replacement and desmopressin.

Hemophilia B is treated with factor IX replacement.

Persistent PTT after factor VIII therapy suggests factor VIII inhibitors.
